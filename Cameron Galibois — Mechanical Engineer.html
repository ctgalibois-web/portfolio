001 / Engineering

Avionics

Avionics Bay

For the team's rocket competing in IREC 2025, I was tasked with developing a housing system for the electronics of the rocket. The project was presented to me with a length constraint and component requirements, and I designed around these necessities to create a lightweight and industry standard solution.

The bay was designed in an Onshape part studio and manufactured using a combination of techniques. I designed the profile of the bulkheads in Onshape, and they were then analyzed using FEA in Ansys. I created a toolpath for and machined the blackbox using a CNC mill. The overall assembly underwent multiple iterations as the electronics developed throughout the year, requiring extensive communication with our electrical engineers. The frequent changes to design were the main motivation in constructing the bay panels using a 3d printer.

3D Printing Avionics Integration Wiring Harness Recovery Systems

03 — Class Projects

Academic Work

A selection of hands-on design and engineering projects completed through Duke's mechanical engineering curriculum.

001

Design Build

Active Suspension Robot

For my capstone project across a semester and a half of classes, myself and groupmates developed an active suspension robot. As a self-proposed project, we assigned ourselves design criteria around impact speed and forces. These criteria were informed by military and NASA experts with whom we met to discuss our project. An actively controlled braking system was developed to apply an appropriate deceleration profile to minimize impact forces transmitted to a payload.

Active Control Large Assembly

002

Design Build

The aMAIZEing Race — State Fair Project

Aiming to design a game of skill for a young audience, my team decided to make a rotatable maze for children to navigate a ball from the top to bottom. I handled the majority of the design work for the team, developing a 3d printable solution consisting of a bearing and shaft interface to connect the maze and base. The parts were joined using threaded heat inserts and fasteners for rigidity and aesthetics.

3D Printing Design to Manufacture

003

Structural Analysis

Pressure Vessel

As a part of my senior year mechanical capstone course, I was tasked with building a pressure vessel. The assignment was simple - design a part to withstand a provided pressure with an assigned fitting. I designed the assembly to minimize machining time and maximize robustness, as weight and material costs were not considered.

FEA Pressure Analysis

004

Fluid Systems

Pump Stand

As a group project during a junior design course, myself and classmates designed a pump stand with the goals of minimizng deflection and weight. The stand needed to withstand a provided torsion and fit into a predetermined mounting bracket.

Class Project Mechanical Design

005

Electromagnetics

Magnetic Levitation

During my Control Systems course, we finished our labs for with a magnetic levitation (Maglev) project. Working with as a team of four, I led the development of a LabView code to orient a PID controller and stabilize an unstable system. A metal ball was levitated to respond to four different inputs - constant, sine, square, and random. The ball responded to each change and mirrored the input signal spontaneously.

Group Work PID LabView
